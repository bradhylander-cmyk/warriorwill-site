Let's break that down piece by piece.

The Float: 33.4M Shares

A 33.4 million share float is tight. When a catalyst hits on a stock this size, even modest buying pressure moves the price significantly. There simply aren't enough shares to absorb demand at the old price.

Short Interest: 17.3% — With 11.64 Days to Cover

17.3% of the float was sold short. That's significant on its own. But the days-to-cover metric is what makes it dangerous for the shorts: 11.64 days means shorts would need almost two full trading weeks to exit at average daily volume. When a catalyst hits and the stock gaps up 57% overnight, they can't wait two weeks. They're forced to buy at market — which pushes the price higher — which forces more short covering. It snowballs.

The March 20 Volume Spike: 3.8x With Only +1.9% Move

🔍 Key Signal

This is the fingerprint. On March 20, volume came in at 3.8× the average — but the stock only moved +1.9%. That's a massive mismatch. When a stock trades nearly 4x normal volume and barely moves, it tells you two things: (1) someone is accumulating, and (2) existing holders are selling into that demand rather than panicking. The result is a coiled spring.

ATR Compression: The $21–$23 Range

For two weeks before the move, $KOD traded in a $21–$23 range. The Average True Range (ATR) was compressing — daily volatility was shrinking even as the broader market moved. Tight consolidation after a quiet volume spike is a classic pre-breakout pattern. Energy was building.

The Catalyst: What clinicaltrials.gov Told Us

The fundamental piece was the most public information imaginable — posted on clinicaltrials.gov for anyone to read, since January 31, 2026.

Kodiak Sciences' GLOW2 trial — testing tarcocimab for diabetic retinopathy — had its primary completion date listed in mid-March 2026. Standard biotech timelines put topline data readouts at 6 to 12 weeks after primary completion. That puts the result window squarely in the mid-March to April 2026 range.

📅 Timeline math: Primary completion listed January 31, 2026 → mid-March primary completion → 6–12 week topline window = March–April readout window. The scanner put this into the catalyst calendar and combined it with the technical signals. That's the edge.

The catalyst information was not hidden. It was on a government website. The edge wasn't secret access — it was systematically reading public data that most traders never look at.

The Method: How to Replicate This Yourself

Here's the exact process the scanner follows each night:

  1. Pull trial completion dates from clinicaltrials.gov
    Filter for biotech trials with primary completion dates in the next 6–12 weeks. These are the catalyst windows. This data is public, structured, and updated regularly.
  2. Cross-reference with float data
    Find the matching ticker for each trial. Check float size — under 50M is the threshold, under 10M is high priority.
  3. Check short interest and days-to-cover
    SI above 10% + DTC above 5 = squeeze potential. Both numbers are reported bi-monthly via FINRA and available through most data providers.
  4. Scan for quiet volume anomalies
    Any day with 2× or more average volume where price moved less than 3% gets flagged. This is the accumulation signal.
  5. Check ATR compression
    If the stock has been trading in a narrowing range for 5+ trading days, it's coiling. Add it to the watchlist.
  6. Generate the pre-move fingerprint score
    Each signal adds weight. When a ticker hits all five, it's flagged for the nightly report.

That's it. No black box. No secret algorithm. Systematic reading of public data, combined nightly.

A quiet volume spike is when a stock trades significantly above its average daily volume (2× or more) while the price barely moves (less than 3%). It suggests accumulation — buyers absorbing supply without moving the price, which is often a sign of informed or institutional positioning ahead of a catalyst.
